# Kindle Publishing Metadata Research

Complete guide to metadata requirements for successful Amazon KDP publishing.

## Overview

Publishing on Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) requires much more than just the book content. This research document covers all metadata fields, best practices for book descriptions, keywords, categories, and marketing information needed for successful ebook publishing.

---

## 1. Required KDP Metadata Fields

### Essential (Cannot Publish Without)

1. **Book Title**
   - Must match book cover exactly
   - Maximum 200 characters (combined with subtitle)
   - Cannot include HTML tags, unauthorized references, or sales rank mentions
   - Must not be punctuation-only

2. **Author Name**
   - Can use pen name
   - Must not impair customer buying decisions
   - Add additional contributors (editors, illustrators) as optional

3. **Primary Marketplace**
   - Select where majority of sales expected
   - Determines available categories

4. **Primary Audience / Content Declaration**
   - Must indicate if book contains sexually explicit content (Yes/No)
   - Required for category selection
   - Books flagged as sexually explicit removed from Children's/YA categories

5. **AI-Generated Content Disclosure** (as of 2024)
   - Required to inform KDP if AI generated text, images, or translations
   - Applies to new books and updates to existing books

### Highly Recommended (Not Required But Critical)

6. **Book Description**
   - Maximum 4,000 characters (use them all!)
   - This is your book's sales pitch
   - See Section 2 for detailed best practices

7. **Keywords**
   - 7 keyword boxes, up to 50 characters each
   - Critical for discoverability
   - See Section 3 for strategy

8. **Categories**
   - Select up to 3 categories
   - Use BISAC codes (though Amazon uses own system)
   - See Section 4 for selection strategy

9. **Book Cover Image**
   - Must match metadata fields
   - Cannot mislead or mimic other book designs

### Optional But Valuable

10. **Subtitle**
    - Maximum 200 characters (combined with title)
    - Clarifies book's focus or adds intrigue

11. **Series Information**
    - Series name
    - Series number
    - Helps readers find related books

12. **Reading Age** (for children's/YA books)
    - Optional age range recommendation
    - Customers can also provide feedback on reading age

13. **ISBN**
    - Required for print books
    - Optional for ebooks (KDP provides free ISBN)
    - Can provide your own if you have one

## 2. Book Description Best Practices

### Character Limits
- **Maximum**: 4,000 characters (Amazon's hard limit)
- **Optimal**: 150-300 words (~800-1,600 characters)
- **Key principle**: Be compelling and scannable, not exhaustive
- Shorter, focused descriptions often convert better than long ones

### Core Structure

#### For Fiction:

**1. Hook (First 1-2 sentences)**
   - Grab attention immediately
   - Set tone and genre
   - First line crucial (visible before "read more" on mobile)
   - Examples:
     - "She has one week to find her sister's killerâ€”or become the next victim."
     - "In a world where memories can be stolen, one thief refuses to forget."

**2. Main Conflict/Stakes (2 paragraphs)**
   - Introduce protagonist
   - Present central conflict
   - Raise emotional stakes
   - DO NOT spoil plot twists
   - Use "they" or character names, avoid "you"
   - Focus on: What does the character want? What's stopping them? What happens if they fail?

**3. Mood/Theme Keywords**
   - Naturally integrate genre keywords
   - Include mood adjectives (haunting, thrilling, heartwarming)
   - Mention themes (redemption, betrayal, survival)

**4. Comparable Titles (Optional)**
   - "Fans of [X] will love..."
   - "Perfect for readers who enjoyed [Y]"
   - Use recent titles (last 5 years), not mega-bestsellers

**5. Call to Action**
   - Simple ending encouraging purchase
   - "Discover..." / "Experience..." / "Join [character] on..."

#### For Nonfiction:

**1. Hook (Problem Statement)**
   - Identify reader's pain point or challenge
   - Use "you" to make it personal
   - Make reader feel understood

**2. Solution (Your Book)**
   - Position book as the answer
   - Focus on BENEFITS, not features
   - What will reader gain/achieve/learn?

**3. Credentials**
   - Establish authority
   - Mention relevant expertise, awards, previous works
   - More critical for nonfiction than fiction

**4. Social Proof (If Available)**
   - Awards, bestseller status
   - NOT reviews/testimonials (forbidden as of May 2024)

**5. Call to Action**
   - Encourage transformation
   - "Learn how to..." / "Discover the secrets..."

### Formatting Guidelines

**Allowed HTML Tags:**
- `<br>` - Line breaks
- `<p>` - Paragraphs
- `<b>` - Bold text (use for hook, CTAs, key phrases)
- `<em>` or `<i>` - Italics
- `<u>` - Underline
- `<h4>` to `<h6>` - Headers (use sparingly)
- `<ol>` and `<ul>` - Numbered/bulleted lists

**Formatting Best Practices:**
- **Bold your hook** - First sentence in bold
- **Short paragraphs** - 2-3 sentences maximum
- **Visual breaks** - Use spacing to create scannable sections
- **Mix formatting** - Bold, italics, breaks for visual hierarchy
- **Mobile-friendly** - Most readers shop on phones

### What NOT to Include

**Strictly Forbidden:**
- Reviews, quotes, or testimonials (as of May 2024)
- Contact information (email, website, social media)
- Promotional content (sales, discounts, time-sensitive info)
- Spoilers
- Unicode emojis
- Keyword stuffing or book tags
- Pornographic or offensive content
- Amazon program names ("Kindle Unlimited", "KDP Select")

### Description Writing Checklist

- [ ] First sentence is compelling hook
- [ ] Genre clearly indicated
- [ ] Main conflict/problem presented
- [ ] Stakes are clear
- [ ] Emotional connection established
- [ ] Keywords naturally integrated
- [ ] Formatting used for visual hierarchy
- [ ] Under 4,000 characters
- [ ] Proofread for errors
- [ ] No forbidden content
- [ ] Sounds similar to successful books in genre
- [ ] Ends with call to action

## 3. Keywords Strategy

### The Basics

**Amazon gives you 7 keyword boxes:**
- Each box can hold up to 50 characters
- Can be single words or phrases
- Phrases should be 2-3 words long
- Keywords are NOT visible to customers

### Keyword Selection Strategy

**1. Start with Amazon Search**
   - Use Amazon's search bar autocomplete
   - Type your genre + topic, see what Amazon suggests
   - These suggestions = what readers actually search for

**2. Target Specificity**
   - Specific words beat general words
   - "space opera romance" > "science fiction"
   - "cozy mystery bakery" > "mystery"

**3. Balance Volume and Competition**
   - High search volume = more potential readers
   - Low competition = easier to rank
   - Use tools like Publisher Rocket (paid) or Keyword Tool Dominator (free)

**4. Category-Reinforcing Keywords**
   - Use 1-2 of your 7 keywords to target/reinforce categories
   - Helps Amazon understand your book's niche
   - Example: "urban fantasy werewolves" reinforces Urban Fantasy category

**5. Use All 7 Keyword Boxes**
   - Don't leave any empty
   - Each box is an opportunity for discovery

### What to AVOID in Keywords

- Don't use quotes (limits to exact phrase match)
- Don't include your book title or author name (redundant)
- Don't use Amazon program names (Kindle Unlimited, KDP Select)
- Don't repeat keywords across boxes
- Don't use category names (use actual search terms)
- Don't use trademarked terms

### Keyword Research Tools

**Free:**
- Amazon search autocomplete
- Amazon "Customers who bought this also bought"
- Keyword Tool Dominator (Amazon Books department)

**Paid:**
- Publisher Rocket (formerly KDP Rocket) - Most popular
- KDP Spy
- Book Bolt

### Example Keyword Strategy (Urban Fantasy)

Box 1: `urban fantasy romance paranormal`
Box 2: `witches magic supernatural thriller`
Box 3: `strong female protagonist fantasy`
Box 4: `dark fantasy urban setting`
Box 5: `enemies to lovers paranormal romance`
Box 6: `fast paced action adventure fantasy`
Box 7: `magical realism contemporary fantasy`

## 4. Categories & BISAC Codes

### Understanding the Systems

**BISAC Codes:**
- Industry standard classification (Book Industry Study Group)
- Used by libraries, wholesalers, databases
- You select up to 3 BISAC codes
- NOT visible on Amazon
- As of June 2023, Amazon uses its own category system

**Amazon Categories:**
- Amazon's own proprietary system
- More specific than BISAC
- Visible to customers
- Don't need to match BISAC exactly

### Selecting Categories

**Basic Rules:**
1. Choose up to **3 categories**
2. Be as **specific** as possible
3. Must accurately reflect book's **central storyline/topic**
4. Wrong categories = poor customer experience = lower sales

**Strategic Selection:**

**Option 1: Broad Reach**
- Choose 3 different category paths
- Example: Fiction > Fantasy > Urban, Fiction > Romance > Paranormal, Fiction > Action & Adventure

**Option 2: Niche Domination**
- Choose 3 very specific subcategories
- Less competitive = easier to become "bestseller" in that category
- Example: Fiction > Fantasy > Urban > Vampires

**Option 3: Hybrid**
- 1 broad category for visibility
- 2 specific subcategories for bestseller badges

### Finding the Right Categories

**Methods:**
1. **Browse Bestseller Lists**
   - Find successful books similar to yours
   - Check their categories (shown on product page)
   - If your book fits there, use those categories

2. **Use Category Browser**
   - Browse Amazon's category tree
   - Look for categories where your book would rank well

3. **Consider Competition**
   - Check bestseller rank of #1 in category
   - Lower bestseller rank = more competitive category
   - Find the sweet spot: relevant + achievable

### BISAC Category Levels

BISAC codes have hierarchical structure:
- **Level 1**: Broad (Fiction, Nonfiction, Juvenile)
- **Level 2**: Genre (Science Fiction, Mystery, Romance)
- **Level 3**: Subgenre (Space Opera, Cozy Mystery, Paranormal Romance)

**Example BISAC Path:**
`FIC028030` = Fiction > Science Fiction > Space Opera

### Important Notes

- Categories can be changed after publishing
- You can request additional categories by contacting KDP support
- Maximum 10 categories total (3 in dashboard + 7 via support)
- Keywords can help you appear in additional categories

## 5. Author Bio Best Practices

### Purpose
- Establish credibility (especially for nonfiction)
- Help readers connect with you
- Encourage them to follow/read more of your work

### Length
- **Optimal**: 100-200 words
- **Minimum**: 50 words
- **Maximum**: No strict limit, but keep it engaging

### What to Include

**For Fiction Authors:**
1. Writing style or themes you explore
2. Previous publications (if any)
3. Awards or recognition
4. Brief personal detail (location, interests related to writing)
5. Where readers can find you (website, social media)
6. What you're working on next

**For Nonfiction Authors:**
1. Professional credentials
2. Expertise and authority on subject
3. Previous publications
4. Awards or media appearances
5. Why you're qualified to write this book
6. Brief personal connection to topic

### Tone
- Match your book's voice
- Third person is traditional but first person can work
- Professional but personable
- Avoid being too formal or too casual

### Example Structure (Fiction)

> [Author Name] writes [genre] novels that explore themes of [themes]. [His/Her/Their] debut novel [Title] won [award/recognition] and has been praised by [publication/author]. When not writing, [Author] [relevant hobby/interest] and lives in [location] with [family/pets]. Visit [website] for news about upcoming releases.

### Example Structure (Nonfiction)

> [Author Name] is a [credentials/title] with over [X years] experience in [field]. [He/She/They] has [relevant achievement] and regularly contributes to [publications/platforms]. [Author's] expertise in [topic] stems from [personal connection/professional experience]. [He/She/They] holds [degrees] from [institutions] and currently [what they do]. Learn more at [website].

### What to AVOID
- Long lists of publications (pick best 2-3)
- Irrelevant personal details
- Negativity or complaints
- Overly promotional language
- Lies or exaggerations

## 6. Comparable Titles (Comp Titles)

### Purpose
- Help readers understand what your book is like
- Position your book in the market
- Attract readers of similar books

### Where to Use
- In book description (optional)
- In marketing materials
- When pitching to agents/publishers (for traditionally published)

### Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use recent books (published in last 3-5 years)
- Choose books with similar:
  - Tone/mood
  - Themes
  - Target audience
  - Length/complexity
- Pick books that are successful but not mega-bestsellers
- Use 2-3 comp titles maximum
- Ensure books are in same genre

**DON'T:**
- Compare to Harry Potter, Twilight, or other mega-franchises
- Use books published more than 5 years ago
- Choose books significantly more famous than your target
- Use comps just because they're popular (must be genuinely similar)
- Compare yourself to "the greats" (Stephen King, J.K. Rowling, etc.)

### How to Find Comp Titles

1. **Amazon "Customers Also Bought"**
   - Look at books similar to yours
   - Check what readers of those books also purchased

2. **Goodreads Lists**
   - Search for lists related to your genre/themes
   - Find recent books with similar descriptions

3. **Amazon Bestseller Lists**
   - Browse relevant categories
   - Look for books ranking well that match your book's vibe

4. **Book Retailers**
   - Check "similar books" recommendations
   - Note which books are grouped together

### Phrasing Comp Titles

**In Description:**
- "Perfect for fans of [Title] and [Title]"
- "Readers who loved [Title] will devour this"
- "If you enjoyed [Title], you'll love [Your Book]"
- "Blending the [aspect] of [Title] with the [aspect] of [Title]"

**Examples:**
- "Perfect for fans of *Red Rising* and *The Hunger Games*"
- "Readers who loved *The Night Circus* will devour this darkly magical tale"
- "Blending the political intrigue of *Game of Thrones* with the space opera adventure of *The Expanse*"

## 7. Pricing Strategy

### Royalty Options

**70% Royalty:**
- **Requirements**:
  - Price between $2.99 - $9.99
  - Must be 20% below any print edition price
  - Available in select territories: US, UK, DE, FR, IT, ES, CA, AU, BR, JP, MX, IN
- **Best for**: Most fiction, mid-range nonfiction
- **Maximizes earnings** on books in sweet spot price range

**35% Royalty:**
- **Applies when**:
  - Price below $2.99 or above $9.99
  - Sales in non-70% territories
- **Best for**:
  - Loss-leader pricing ($0.99 first in series)
  - Premium nonfiction ($9.99+)

### Pricing Psychology

**Common Price Points:**
- **$0.99** - Loss leader, series starter, promotion
- **$2.99** - Minimum for 70% royalty, budget conscious readers
- **$3.99-$4.99** - Sweet spot for most fiction
- **$5.99-$7.99** - Longer fiction, established authors
- **$8.99-$9.99** - Maximum 70% royalty price
- **$10.99+** - Premium nonfiction, specialized topics (35% royalty)

### Factors to Consider

1. **Genre expectations**
   - Romance/Mystery: typically $2.99-$4.99
   - Sci-fi/Fantasy: often $4.99-$6.99
   - Literary fiction: $5.99-$8.99
   - Nonfiction: $7.99-$14.99+

2. **Length**
   - Novella (20-50k words): $2.99-$3.99
   - Novel (50-100k words): $3.99-$5.99
   - Epic (100k+ words): $5.99-$7.99

3. **Author platform**
   - Unknown author: price competitively
   - Established author: can command higher prices

4. **Series strategy**
   - Book 1: Lower price to hook readers
   - Subsequent books: Standard genre pricing

### Territory Rights

**Worldwide Rights:**
- Select "All territories" if you hold rights globally
- Your book available on all Amazon marketplaces

**Limited Rights:**
- Select "Individual territories"
- Choose specific countries where you hold rights
- Limits where customers can purchase

**Important**: Only claim territories where you actually hold publishing rights

## 8. Additional Metadata Fields

### Series Information

**When Applicable:**
- Multi-book series (planned or in progress)
- Companion novels in same universe

**Required Fields:**
- **Series Name**: Consistent across all books
- **Series Number**: Position in reading order (can use 0 for prequels)

**Benefits:**
- Helps readers find other books in series
- Amazon recommends next book after reading
- Series pages aggregate all books

### Language

- Primary language of your book
- Amazon auto-detects but you can specify
- Important for international marketplaces

### Publication Date

- Can set future date for pre-orders
- Or publish immediately
- Date appears on product page

### Book Length

**Approximate Page Count:**
- Calculated from word count
- Not exact (varies by device/font)
- Gives readers sense of book length

**Word Count Estimates by Category:**
- Flash Fiction: < 1,000 words
- Short Story: 1,000-7,500 words
- Novelette: 7,500-20,000 words
- Novella: 20,000-50,000 words
- Novel: 50,000-110,000 words
- Epic Novel: 110,000+ words

## 9. Marketing & Discovery Metadata

### What Makes Books Discoverable

1. **Keywords** - How readers find you through search
2. **Categories** - How readers browse to find you
3. **Description** - What converts browsers into buyers
4. **Cover** - First impression, genre signal
5. **Title/Subtitle** - Searchable, memorable, genre-appropriate
6. **Price** - Competitive for your genre/length
7. **Reviews** - Social proof (can't control but critical)

### KDP Select Enrollment

**What it is:**
- Makes your ebook exclusive to Amazon
- Cannot sell on other platforms (Apple Books, Kobo, etc.)

**Benefits:**
- Access to KDP Select Global Fund (Kindle Unlimited)
- Earn money when KU subscribers read your book
- Free promotional tools (Free Book Promotion, Countdown Deals)
- Access to 70% royalty in more territories

**Drawbacks:**
- Exclusivity limits your market
- Not ideal if you have strong following on other platforms

**Recommendation:**
- Try KDP Select for first 90 days (can opt out after)
- See if Kindle Unlimited reads make up for lost sales elsewhere

### A+ Content (Previously Enhanced Brand Content)

**What it is:**
- Enhanced product page with additional images/text
- Appears below main description
- Available for authors enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry

**Not covered in sidecar file but worth knowing about**

## 10. Quality Control Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

### Metadata Accuracy
- [ ] Title matches cover exactly
- [ ] Author name is correct (including any pen name)
- [ ] Description is compelling and error-free
- [ ] All 7 keyword boxes filled strategically
- [ ] 3 categories selected (relevant and strategic)
- [ ] Price appropriate for genre/length
- [ ] Territories correctly selected
- [ ] Series information complete (if applicable)
- [ ] AI-generated content disclosed (if applicable)

### Description Quality
- [ ] Under 4,000 characters
- [ ] Hook is compelling
- [ ] Genre clearly indicated
- [ ] No forbidden content (reviews, contact info, etc.)
- [ ] Formatting used effectively
- [ ] Proofread multiple times
- [ ] Mobile-friendly (short paragraphs)

### Keywords
- [ ] All 7 boxes used
- [ ] No quotes around phrases
- [ ] No repetition across boxes
- [ ] Specific rather than general
- [ ] Research-backed (not guesses)

### Categories
- [ ] Accurately reflect book content
- [ ] As specific as possible
- [ ] Strategic balance of reach and competition

### Author Bio
- [ ] Establishes credibility
- [ ] Appropriate length
- [ ] No errors or typos
- [ ] Includes website/social (optional)

## Summary: Essential Metadata for Successful Publishing

**The Big Three:**
1. **Description** - Your sales pitch (4,000 characters)
2. **Keywords** - Your discoverability (7 boxes of 50 characters)
3. **Categories** - Your position in market (3 categories)

**Everything else supports these three critical elements.**

Get these right, and you maximize your book's chances of finding readers. Get them wrong, and even a great book will struggle to sell.
